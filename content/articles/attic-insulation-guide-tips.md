## Air Sealing Checklist (DO THIS FIRST)

- [ ] Recessed can lights: upgraded to IC-AT rated or boxed with drywall and fire caulk
- [ ] Plumbing vent pipes: sealed with high-temp silicone around PVC; aluminum flashing + high-temp caulk around metal flues
- [ ] Wiring penetrations: spray foam or fire caulk in every drilled hole
- [ ] Attic hatch: foam weatherstrip on perimeter + 2-inch R-10 rigid foam board on attic side
- [ ] Bathroom exhaust fans: ducted to exterior (NOT into attic) and sealed at the ceiling penetration
- [ ] Chimney chase: sealed with metal flashing (NEVER spray foam or caulk directly on a hot chimney)
- [ ] Drop soffits above cabinets: sealed at the top with rigid foam + spray foam
- [ ] Top plates of interior walls: caulk or foam where drywall meets the top plate (visible from attic)

**Cost:** $50-$100. **Time:** 4-8 hours. **Savings:** 50% of total energy improvement.

## The 5 Fatal Attic Insulation Mistakes

1. **Skipping air sealing.** Blowing R-49 insulation over unsealed recessed lights and plumbing vents is like putting on a down jacket without zipping it. 30-50% of the insulation's R-value is wasted.

2. **Blocking soffit vents.** Every rafter bay with a soffit vent needs a baffle ($2 each) to maintain airflow. Without it, blown insulation fills the soffit, stops attic ventilation, and rots the roof deck in 3-5 years. This is the most common DIY error and the most expensive to fix ($3,000-$8,000 for roof deck replacement).

3. **Burying non-IC recessed lights.** Old can lights need 3 inches of clearance on all sides. Insulation in direct contact with a non-IC fixture can start a fire. Upgrade to IC-AT LED retrofit kits ($25-$40 each) before insulating.

4. **Stepping between joists instead of on them.** Joists are 2x6 or 2x8 framing members spaced 16 or 24 inches apart. Walk ONLY on joists or plywood laid across joists. One step between joists goes through the drywall ceiling into the room below. Average repair cost: $500. Average ER visit cost for a fall through the ceiling: much more.

5. **Not overfilling for cellulose settling.** Cellulose settles 13-18% in the first year. If you blow exactly 14 inches for R-49, you'll have R-40 by next winter. Overfill by 2-3 inches. The R-value chart on the GreenFiber bag already accounts for settling — follow it, don't do your own math.

## When to Hire a Pro

- Knob-and-tube wiring present (pre-1950 homes) — fire hazard if buried in insulation. Requires electrician evaluation first.
- Vermiculite insulation present (pre-1990) — may contain asbestos (Zonolite brand from Libby, Montana mine). Do NOT disturb. Have it tested ($50 mail-in kit). If positive, professional asbestos abatement required ($3,000-$8,000).
- Roof leaks present — fix before insulating. Wet cellulose collapses ceilings.
- Cannot physically work in attic (cramped space, extreme heat, limited mobility) — professional installation costs $2,000-$4,000. The DIY savings are substantial but only if you can safely do the work.
