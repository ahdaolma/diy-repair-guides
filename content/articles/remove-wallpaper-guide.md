## Why Wallpaper Removal Is Difficult
![Scoring wallpaper with a perforation tool](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse3.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.JRg3u4uTK4fEMzGU1NGIEAHaG0%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=47bd8ecb1f99dfbddd39197550b7fcfb6004ac019b2a9b232f7c15be6a8a7431&ipo=images)

Wallpaper removal difficulty depends on three factors: the wallpaper type, the wall preparation before it was hung, and your patience. Vinyl-coated wallpaper (peelable top layer) is easier—the vinyl face peels off dry, leaving paper backing to soak and scrape. Solid paper wallpaper absorbs water better. Fabric-backed or grasscloth wallpaper is the most difficult. The best-case scenario: the installer sized the walls (applied a primer-sealer) before hanging, which prevents the adhesive from bonding directly to the drywall. The worst case: wallpaper hung directly on unprimed drywall, where scraping tears the paper face of the drywall.

Before committing to removal, test a corner. If the wallpaper peels off in large sheets, it was properly sized and will come off cleanly. If it tears off in shreds and takes drywall paper with it, you are in for a fight—consider skim-coating over the wallpaper with joint compound instead of removing it.

## Method 1: Chemical Remover (Best for Most Wallpapers)
![Steaming wallpaper off a wall](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP._x6eHOSOaiEnnL_ZC3-STAHaEl%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=e295cb5924815782c857a8bf5732fdd5dde30fb940a60e020b06deb85314d0b9&ipo=images)

1. Protect the floor with plastic drop cloths taped to the baseboards. Water and chemical remover will drip.
2. Score the wallpaper with a scoring tool. Roll it over the entire wall surface in a circular motion, applying even pressure. The tool creates hundreds of tiny perforations that allow liquid to reach the adhesive layer. Do not press so hard that you perforate the drywall beneath—you should feel the teeth puncturing the wallpaper but not digging into the wall.
3. Mix chemical wallpaper remover (DIF concentrate, enzyme-based) with hot water per the label. Apply to the wall with a sponge, spray bottle, or garden pump sprayer. Work in 4×4 foot sections—the remover needs to stay wet to work.
4. Let the remover dwell for 15–20 minutes. Reapply if the wall dries out. The enzymes break down the starch-based adhesive.
5. Starting at a seam or a perforated area, lift the wallpaper with a putty knife and peel. Vinyl wallpapers often release their top layer in large sheets, leaving paper backing. Apply more remover to the backing, wait 10 minutes, and scrape.
6. Scrape with a 6-inch drywall knife held at a low angle (30 degrees or less). A steep angle gouges the drywall.
7. After removing all wallpaper, wash the walls with warm water and a sponge to remove residual adhesive. The wall should feel smooth, not tacky, when dry.
8. Let the walls dry completely (24–48 hours with a fan) before priming and painting.

## Method 2: Steamer (Best for Stubborn, Multi-Layer, or Unperforated Wallpaper)

A wallpaper steamer uses a reservoir of boiling water connected by a hose to a flat steaming plate held against the wall. The steam penetrates the wallpaper and dissolves the adhesive.

1. Score the wallpaper as in Method 1. Steam penetrates perforations faster.
2. Fill the steamer and let it come to a full boil.
3. Hold the steam plate against the wall for 15–30 seconds per section. Move the plate to the adjacent section while you scrape the steamed area—the wallpaper needs to be scraped while the adhesive is still hot and wet. Once it cools, the adhesive re-hardens.
4. Use a 6-inch drywall knife to scrape. The wallpaper should release from the wall in strips. If it tears in small pieces, steam each section longer.
5. Work in a systematic pattern—top to bottom, left to right.
6. Wear gloves and long sleeves. The steam plate is hot, and hot water drips from it. Drop cloths are essential—the steamer produces a surprising amount of water that pools on the floor.

**Steamer safety:** Never overfill the reservoir. Keep the steamer upright—tipping spills boiling water. Unplug when not in active use. Do not leave a hot steamer unattended around children or pets.

## Method 3: The Fabric Softener Trick
![Scraping wallpaper off a wall with putty knife](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse4.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.VrNc546prH-AyGeTjfiJKHaDP%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=770192dea96a6833680356cd0089b1ec3638a770187ccaf443a5699c5b1ab8e9&ipo=images)

A mixture of liquid fabric softener and hot water (1:3 ratio) is a DIY chemical remover. The surfactants in fabric softener break down wallpaper adhesive similarly to commercial enzyme removers. Mix in a spray bottle, score the wallpaper, spray liberally, wait 15–20 minutes, and scrape. This method costs under $2 and works well on solid paper wallpaper. It is less effective on vinyl-coated wallpaper because the waterproof vinyl layer prevents the solution from reaching the adhesive—peel the vinyl face off first if possible.

## After Removal: Wall Preparation

1. Wash the walls thoroughly with TSP (trisodium phosphate) or a TSP substitute to remove all residual adhesive. Sponge, rinse with clean water, and repeat. The walls must be completely clean—any adhesive residue will react with primer and paint, causing it to bubble or peel.
2. Repair any drywall damage: gouges from the scraper, torn drywall paper, exposed gypsum. Seal torn drywall paper with an oil-based primer (Zinsser BIN or Kilz Original)—water-based primer will cause the exposed paper to bubble. Skim-coat gouges with joint compound, sand smooth, and spot-prime.
3. Apply a high-quality drywall primer-sealer (not paint-and-primer-in-one—a dedicated primer). This seals any residual adhesive, prevents stains, and provides a uniform surface for paint. Zinsser Gardz is specifically formulated for problematic surfaces after wallpaper removal.
4. Let the primer dry fully. Inspect the walls under raking light for imperfections. Fix them now—paint highlights, not hides.
