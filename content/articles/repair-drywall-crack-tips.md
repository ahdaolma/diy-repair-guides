## Crack Diagnosis at a Glance

| Crack Location | Width | Likely Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Straight, at seam | Hairline to 1/16" | Normal settling, tape failure | Retape |
| Corner (wall/ceiling) | 1/16" to 1/8" | Truss uplift (seasonal) | Retape with paper tape |
| Diagonal from door/window corner | 1/16" to 1/8" | Framing stress concentration | Retape |
| Over 1/8", one side higher | 1/8"+ | Foundation movement | **Structural engineer** |
| Horizontal, mid-wall | Any width | Foundation/retaining wall issue | **Structural engineer** |
| With water stain | Any width | Active leak | **Fix leak first** |

## Tape Guide

| Tape Type | Best For | NEVER Use For |
|---|---|---|
| Paper tape ($6) | Flat seams, inside corners, crack repair | Outside corners without metal reinforcement |
| Fiberglass mesh ($8) | Small patch repairs | Ceilings, high-stress cracks (too flexible) |
| Metal-reinforced paper ($12) | Outside corners | Flat seams |

Paper tape is stronger than mesh for bridging cracks. Use paper tape for ALL crack repairs.

## Setting Compound Types

| Product | Set Time | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Easy Sand 45 | 45 min | Small repairs, need fast turnaround |
| Easy Sand 90 | 90 min | Larger repairs, more working time |
| Durabond 90 | 90 min | Deep fills (MUCH harder — difficult to sand) |

Setting compound is for pre-filling wide cracks. It hardens chemically, shrinks less, and is stronger than pre-mixed compound. NEVER use Durabond as a finish coat — it's nearly unsandable.
