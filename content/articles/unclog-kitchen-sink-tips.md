## Try Boiling Water Before Anything Else

![Kitchen sink P-trap disassembly for cleaning](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthfvnext.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.gaXldKglAdOHhVuj_uJR8gHaHQ%3Fcb%3Dthfvnextfalcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=e6a866917c2b15ca040265135089f409fda0234e77372256460ccd2eeac301a8&ipo=images)

For slow-but-flowing drains, boiling water is the most effective first move—and it is free. Bring a full pot to a rolling boil and pour it into the drain in two or three stages, pausing 10–15 seconds between pours. The heat melts and flushes the top layers of grease that line the pipe walls. This alone clears roughly one-third of minor kitchen clogs. Important: if the sink is fully clogged with standing water, bail out as much as possible first. Pouring boiling water into a sink full of cold water loses most of the heat before it reaches the clog.

## Never Use Chemical Cleaners on a Completely Blocked Sink

![Plunger and basic drain cleaning tools](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthfvnext.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.ojbHyaki60zzAX7jUeqWfAHaHa%3Fcb%3Dthfvnextfalcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=d93250ef56d8da65911c3d257f320df10c7d794b5ad52676e7f60597e41cba85&ipo=images)

If water is standing in the sink with zero drainage, chemical cleaners (Drano, Liquid-Plumr) sit in the bowl and do not reach the clog. They create a pool of caustic liquid you will have to bail out by hand before you can remove the P-trap or snake the drain. If you have already poured chemicals in, wear rubber gloves and safety glasses before doing anything else. Better to save chemical cleaners for slow drains that still flow, and even then use them sparingly—sodium hydroxide degrades PVC over time and can accelerate corrosion in metal pipes.

## Seal the Second Basin When Plunging

If you have a double-basin kitchen sink, a plunger will not build pressure against the clog unless you seal the other drain. Stuff a wet rag tightly into the second drain opening (or close the stopper and hold it down). Without this seal, each plunger stroke simply pushes water out the other basin rather than forcing it through the clog. Also remove the sink strainer or basket from the drain you are plunging—the plunger needs to seal directly against the drain opening, not against a strainer.

## Clean the P-Trap Before Assuming the Worst

![Drain snake being fed into wall pipe](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthfvnext.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.O0IEgD37zqdd-on8ZH3f8QHaHA%3Fcb%3Dthfvnextfalcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=95c5b76b26233f456271d45d40bd556aeaf2cf86fb2a318c1869b29f6d2240c3&ipo=images)

The P-trap is the U-shaped pipe directly under the sink and is the clog location in roughly 70% of kitchen sink blockages. You can remove it in 2 minutes with no tools other than your hands (the slip nuts are designed to be hand-tightened). Place a bucket underneath first—the trap is full of water and will spill. Clean the trap with a bottle brush and hot soapy water. Inspect the trap interior for a thick layer of congealed grease and food paste—if you find it, the clog was in the trap and cleaning it solves the problem. Reassemble, run water, and check for drips.

## Snake Through the Wall Pipe, Not the Drain Opening

If the P-trap is clean and the clog persists, feed the drain snake into the wall pipe (after removing the P-trap), not down through the sink drain. Snaking through the drain opening forces the snake through the P-trap, making it harder to advance and risking damage to the trap. Go straight into the wall stub-out. Most kitchen drain lines run horizontally through the wall for several feet before dropping vertically—the clog is typically somewhere in this horizontal run where grease and debris settle.

## Flush With Cold Water When Using the Disposal

The single most effective kitchen drain prevention tip: always run cold water—not hot—when operating the garbage disposal. Hot water melts grease, which then flows into the drain, cools, and solidifies on the pipe walls. Cold water keeps grease in a solid state so the disposal can chop it into small particles that flush through without sticking. Run cold water for a full 10–15 seconds after the disposal sound changes from grinding to a smooth hum, indicating the chamber is clear.

## Throw Coffee Grounds and Eggshells in the Trash

These two items appear in drain clogs constantly because people believe they are harmless. Coffee grounds do not dissolve—they accumulate in the low points of drain lines like sediment in a river, forming a dense sludge that traps grease and other debris. Eggshells have a thin membrane that wraps around the disposal blades and a hard shell that breaks into sand-like grit that settles in the P-trap. Both belong in the trash or compost, never the disposal. Potato peels, pasta, and rice are equally problematic—they swell with water and form a starchy paste that coats pipes.

## Install a Mesh Strainer and Use It Relentlessly

A $3 stainless steel mesh sink strainer catches food particles before they enter the drain. Get one with a rim that sits flush in the drain opening so debris cannot sneak around the edges. After every meal, empty the strainer into the trash—do not tap it out over the sink. This single habit eliminates the vast majority of kitchen clogs because it stops the food particles that form the physical matrix of the clog, without which grease alone drains slowly but rarely blocks a pipe entirely.
