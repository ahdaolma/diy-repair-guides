## Ladder and Roof Safety—Read This First
![Damaged asphalt shingle being replaced](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthfvnext.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.DBEdDo-oroj5jFlCPSuIdQHaE8%3Fcb%3Dthfvnextfalcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=fb463cac9e1c46740760caa555480591cc5ad36f09e669944d1f35047fd55a55&ipo=images)

Working on a roof is dangerous. Falls from roofs account for a significant percentage of fatal home accidents. Follow these rules:

- Use an extension ladder that extends at least 3 feet above the roof edge. Secure it at the top.
- Do not work on a wet, icy, or windy roof. Asphalt shingles are extremely slippery when wet.
- Wear soft-soled shoes (running shoes or deck shoes) for traction. Do not wear work boots with stiff soles—they slip on shingle granules.
- Use a safety harness anchored to a roof anchor if the roof pitch is steeper than 6:12 (rises 6 inches per 12 inches horizontal—about 26 degrees) or if the roof is higher than one story.
- Have a spotter on the ground with a cell phone.
- Be aware of power lines. Keep aluminum ladders and tools well clear of the service drop to the house.

If you are not comfortable on a roof, hire a roofer. Single shingle repair: $150–$300. It is not worth a fall.

## Diagnose the Damage
![Lifting shingle tab to access roofing nails](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthfvnext.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.nX307SGqnKR_MlDYZuKXPAHaMD%3Fcb%3Dthfvnextfalcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=8fc9497df7f86f6131fbfbb83b21350a7039e8a476b15690e831c95ab2e0d632&ipo=images)

- **Missing shingle (blown off):** Wind lifted the shingle tab, broke the seal strip, and tore the shingle at the nail line. Replace the shingle.
- **Curled or cupped shingle:** The shingle is delaminating or has lost flexibility with age. Replace the shingle. Gluing it down with roofing cement is a temporary fix at best.
- **Cracked shingle (impact, tree branch):** Replace.
- **Granule loss (bald spots, visible asphalt):** The shingle is at end of life. If multiple shingles show granule loss across the roof, plan for full replacement within 1–3 years. Patching individual bald shingles does not stop the underlying deterioration.
- **Multiple shingles missing or damaged across the roof:** The roof is failing systemically. Replacing individual shingles is a holding action before full replacement.

## Materials

- Matching replacement shingles (take a damaged shingle to the roofing supply store for matching—architectural shingles come in dozens of colors)
- Roofing nails (1¼-inch, galvanized, large-head)
- Flat pry bar (for lifting shingle tabs without tearing)
- Hammer
- Utility knife with hook blades
- Roofing cement (in a caulk tube, not a bucket)
- Caulk gun

## Repair 1: Replace a Single Damaged Shingle
![Applying roofing cement to seal a shingle repair](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthfvnext.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.SRX0Ntyn5NzYqe1Y1d9XZgHaDK%3Fcb%3Dthfvnextfalcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=3004014dce4828ff6afdc9eb2ddaf1340967d70b5d350131d6702a6e125475f5&ipo=images)

1. Gently lift the shingle tab two rows above the damaged shingle. Slide a flat pry bar under the tab and lift just enough to access the nails below. Work the tabs loose from their seal strips carefully—you will need to re-seal them.
2. Under the tabs, locate the nails holding the damaged shingle in place. There are typically four nails per shingle—two in the shingle itself and two in the shingle below whose nails penetrate through.
3. Use the pry bar to gently lift and remove the nails from the damaged shingle. Slide the bar under the nail head and lever it up. Remove all nails.
4. Slide the damaged shingle out from under the tabs above it. If it tears, remove all pieces.
5. If the nails from the shingle two rows above penetrate through the replacement area, you may need to cut them flush from below with the pry bar—do not pull these nails, as they secure the shingle above.
6. Slide the new shingle into place, aligning it with the adjacent shingles in the row.
7. Nail the new shingle: drive 1¼-inch roofing nails approximately 1 inch above the cutout slots (the notches), just below the seal strip, and about 1 inch from each edge—four nails per shingle. Nail heads must be covered by the shingle tab two rows above.
8. Press the lifted shingle tabs back down onto the seal strip. Apply a dab of roofing cement under each tab corner that was lifted to re-establish the wind seal. The sun's heat will reactivate the seal strip over the next warm day.
9. Apply roofing cement to the exposed nail heads for extra waterproofing if any are visible.

## Repair 2: Re-Seal Curled Shingles

For shingles that are intact but curling and lifting:

1. On a warm day (above 60°F), the seal strip may reactivate with heat alone. Weigh down the curled corner with a brick wrapped in a cloth for 24 hours on a warm day. The sun softens the asphalt and the weight re-establishes the bond.
2. If heat alone does not work: apply a quarter-sized dab of roofing cement under the curled corner. Press down firmly for 30 seconds. Place a brick on top for 24 hours.
3. Do this only for 1–3 isolated curled shingles. If curling is widespread, the roof is deteriorating.

## When to Replace the Entire Roof

Asphalt shingle roofs have a finite lifespan: 15–25 years for three-tab shingles, 25–40+ years for architectural shingles, depending on climate and ventilation. Signs a full replacement is needed:

- Granule loss across the roof (bald spots on 20%+ of shingles, or granules collecting in gutters by the cupful)
- Curling or cupping on more than a handful of shingles
- Missing shingles after normal (not extreme) wind events
- Visible sagging or soft spots in the roof deck
- The roof is 20+ years old and you are repairing shingles more than once per year

Full roof replacement costs $5,000–$15,000 depending on size, pitch, and material. Multiple patch repairs over several years can exceed the cost of replacement without providing the same protection.
