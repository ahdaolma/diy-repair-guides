## Why Replace a Window Screen
![Removing old spline from window screen frame](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse4.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.NRpLmzndnnjgtjC5nuCWOwHaHa%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=288db5c4abb2651c96b15058c0476cd90c5814e7b188a8365d00f8c29dd460be&ipo=images)

A torn or sagging window screen is not just an eyesore—it lets in mosquitoes, flies, and debris. Replacing the screen mesh on a standard window costs $10–$20 in materials and takes 20–30 minutes per screen. Professional screen repair shops charge $25–$50 per screen for the same materials and labor. If you have multiple screens to repair, the spline roller tool ($5–8) pays for itself on the first screen.

## Materials and Tools

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Screen mesh (fiberglass, charcoal) | $8–12 per roll (25–50 ft) |
| Screen spline (rubber cord, bring old piece to match diameter) | $3–5 per roll |
| Spline roller tool (convex and concave wheels) | $5–8 |
| Utility knife with sharp blade | Already have |
| Flathead screwdriver or awl (to remove old spline) | Already have |
| Scissors | Already have |

**Screen types:**
- **Fiberglass (standard, charcoal color):** Most common. Does not dent, crease, or corrode. Slightly flexible. Best for most homes.
- **Aluminum (silver):** More rigid, dentable, can corrode in coastal environments. Slightly better visibility because the wire is thinner. Used on older homes.
- **Pet-resistant (heavy-duty fiberglass, vinyl-coated polyester):** Thicker mesh that resists clawing and pushing. 2–3x the cost but worth it if you have dogs or cats.
- **Solar screen (denser weave):** Blocks 60–90% of UV rays. Darker appearance from outside. Reduces solar heat gain through windows.

## Step 1: Remove the Screen From the Window

Most window screens are held in place by spring-loaded tension pins or friction-fit in a channel. Press the pins inward or lift the screen tabs to release. Set the screen on a flat work surface—a workbench, sawhorses, or a clean garage floor.

## Step 2: Remove the Old Spline and Screen
![Rolling new spline into screen frame with tool](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse4.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.JCDWqBHWwtfT-tyKz9bNSwHaHa%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=c44a0f974b80f4ee015cfd1f074729bd364a0fee3a81dcb51d67a11eae42c89a&ipo=images)

The spline is the rubber cord pressed into a groove around the frame perimeter that holds the screen mesh in tension.

1. Use a flathead screwdriver or an awl to pick up one end of the old spline. Once you have enough to grip, pull it out of the groove by hand. It usually comes out in one piece.
2. The old screen mesh will now be loose. Remove and discard it.
3. Inspect the spline groove. Remove any stuck spline fragments or debris. Wipe the groove clean.
4. If the old spline is still round and flexible, it can be reused. If it is flat, cracked, or hard, replace it.

## Step 3: Cut and Position the New Screen
![Finished window screen installed in frame](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse2.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.PGVoKwGXH_DhME6QQ-IyCgHaHa%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=26b92c6a94052b4d628179160afb16e45ec470b4e576004dde49854048be464c&ipo=images)

1. Roll out the new screen mesh over the frame. Cut it roughly 2 inches larger than the frame on all four sides. The excess gives you something to grip when stretching.
2. Position the mesh so the weave runs parallel to the frame edges. Diagonal mesh looks sloppy and will not stretch evenly.

## Step 4: Install the Spline

1. Starting in one corner, press the spline into the groove using the concave wheel of the spline roller. The concave wheel pushes the spline and screen into the groove simultaneously.
2. Work your way around the frame, keeping the screen mesh pulled taut as you go. The screen should be flat with no wrinkles or sags as it enters the groove. For large screens, have a helper hold tension on the opposite side.
3. At corners, push the spline firmly into the corner with the roller—do not cut the spline at corners. One continuous piece around the entire perimeter holds tension better than four separate pieces.
4. When you reach the starting corner, cut the spline with a utility knife and press the end into the groove.

## Step 5: Trim the Excess Screen

1. Use a sharp utility knife held flat against the outside edge of the spline groove. Cut away the excess screen mesh by running the blade along the groove edge. The blade rides against the spline, producing a clean cut.
2. Alternatively, use the outer spine of the spline roller to press the mesh edge into the groove as a guide, then cut.
3. Do not cut into the spline itself—just the screen mesh.
4. Reinstall the screen in the window.

## Frame Repair

If the aluminum frame is bent or the corner has separated:

- **Bent frame:** Lay the frame on a flat surface, cover the bent area with a block of wood, and tap gently with a hammer until straight. Aluminum bends easily and work-hardens—minimize the number of bends.
- **Separated corner:** The corners are held by plastic L-shaped corner keys pressed into the hollow frame channels. Remove the spline and screen from the affected corner. Pull the frame pieces apart, insert a new corner key ($2–$4 for a pack of 4 at any hardware store), tap the frame pieces together with a rubber mallet, and rescreen.
- **Hole in frame:** Small holes can be filled with epoxy putty, sanded smooth, and repainted. Large holes or cracks warrant frame replacement—a new frame kit costs $15–$25.
