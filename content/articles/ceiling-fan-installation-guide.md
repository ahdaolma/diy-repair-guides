## The Most Important Safety Rule
![Installing a ceiling fan brace between joists](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.CtyJ0_O65XRE0agXRf1Y3QHaEl%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=c275fb45776e5f9a4080722680358393fa26938471183c2c31df911a9dcd9d8a&ipo=images)

**The electrical box must be rated for ceiling fan support.** A standard plastic or metal octagon box rated for a light fixture (35–50 pounds) is not sufficient for a ceiling fan, which weighs 20–50 pounds and subjects the box to dynamic forces from rotation and vibration. The National Electrical Code (NEC 314.27) requires that ceiling fan boxes be listed and marked for fan support. A fan that falls from the ceiling is a serious injury hazard.

If you are replacing an existing light fixture with a ceiling fan, determine whether the existing box is fan-rated. Look inside the box for a stamp that says "Acceptable for Fan Support" or a weight rating of 70+ pounds. If the box is not fan-rated—or if you cannot determine its rating—install a retrofit fan brace and box.

## Tools and Materials

- Non-contact voltage tester
- Fan-rated retrofit brace and box kit ($15–25)
- Adjustable wrench, Phillips and flathead screwdrivers
- Wire stripper, needle-nose pliers
- Wire nuts (included with fan)
- Step ladder
- Ceiling fan ($50–$300)

## Step 1: Install a Fan-Rated Box (If Needed)
![Wiring connections for ceiling fan installation](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse4.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.-MT6RsZThGpquaWdDvte4AHaEK%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=9811dbd41eecc6cdc8224d5a148e9cdb45ad159b5015b43b354b16b32bbdc057&ipo=images)

A retrofit fan brace (Westinghouse Saf-T-Brace, Raco Retro-Brace) installs through the existing ceiling hole and expands to grip the joists.

1. Turn off the breaker and verify zero voltage.
2. Remove the existing light fixture and the old electrical box. Cut the box free from the ceiling if it is nailed to a joist using a reciprocating saw or hacksaw blade inserted through the hole. Remove the nails.
3. Slide the fan brace into the ceiling hole oriented perpendicular to the joists. The brace has a ratcheting or screw mechanism that expands the ends against the joists. Expand it until it is firmly wedged. Some models have teeth that bite into the joists—you should feel significant resistance when expanding.
4. Attach the new fan-rated metal box to the brace with the included U-bolt and nuts. The box should sit flush with the ceiling surface. Pull the existing house wiring into the box through a knockout.
5. The brace and box assembly should now be rigid enough to support the fan weight without any movement.

## Step 2: Assemble the Fan (On the Ground)

Ceiling fans are easier to assemble on the floor or a workbench than overhead. Follow the manufacturer's instructions, which vary by model. Generally:

1. Attach the mounting bracket to the electrical box using the provided screws.
2. Assemble the fan motor housing, downrod, and canopy. For standard 8-foot ceilings, use the shortest downrod included (typically 3–4 inches). For ceilings over 9 feet, use a longer downrod (sold separately).
3. Feed the fan wires through the downrod and canopy.
4. Attach the blades to the blade brackets, then attach the brackets to the motor. A reversible drill with a clutch set to low torque prevents overtightening the blade screws, which can strip the threaded inserts in the blade brackets.

## Step 3: Wire the Fan
![Assembled ceiling fan with light kit installed](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.EZMZf1EV1O8G3sEXzYtcgAHaHa%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=e7abd8d8f81f1865cbc1c30f938c12b1a442dd53549d59472a69982bbf20f287&ipo=images)

Standard ceiling fan wiring with a light kit and single wall switch:

- **Green (fan) → Bare copper/Green (ceiling):** Ground.
- **White (fan) → White (ceiling):** Neutral for both fan motor and light.
- **Black (fan) → Black (ceiling):** Hot for fan motor.
- **Blue (fan, light kit) → Black (ceiling):** Hot for light kit. Connect to the same black wire as the fan motor if you want the wall switch to control both fan and light together. If separate switches control fan and light, connect blue to the second switched hot wire (typically red).

If the ceiling box has only two wires (black and white), connect both the fan black and blue wires to the ceiling black wire. The fan and light will be controlled by the wall switch together, and you will use the pull chains for independent control.

1. Twist wires together clockwise with pliers (3–4 twists), then wire nut.
2. Tug each wire to confirm the connection is tight.
3. Wrap wire nuts with electrical tape.
4. Fold wires carefully into the box.

## Step 4: Mount the Fan

1. Lift the assembled fan (this is the physically hardest part—a second person helps enormously) and hang the canopy on the mounting bracket hook temporarily. This supports the weight while you make final connections.
2. Complete the wiring if not already done.
3. Lift the canopy onto the mounting bracket and secure with the provided screws. The canopy should sit flush against the ceiling with no gap.

## Step 5: Balance the Blades

A wobbling fan is almost always a balance issue, not a defective fan.

1. Ensure all blade screws are tight and blades are evenly spaced. Even a quarter-turn difference in screw tightness affects balance.
2. Measure the distance from each blade tip to the ceiling. If one blade is higher or lower, gently bend the blade bracket (not the blade itself) until all blades are in the same plane.
3. Use the included blade balancing kit (a plastic clip and weights). Attach the clip to one blade, run the fan on high, and observe wobble. Move the clip to each blade in turn. The blade where the clip reduces wobble the most is the light blade.
4. Attach the adhesive weight to the top of that blade at the midpoint. Fine-tune the position of the weight slightly inboard or outboard until wobble is minimized.
5. If balancing does not resolve severe wobble, the downrod ball may not be properly seated in the mounting bracket—ensure the ball is fully engaged in the bracket socket.

## Maintenance

- Reverse the fan direction seasonally (switch on the motor housing): counterclockwise in summer (pulls cool air up, creates wind chill), clockwise in winter (pushes warm air down from the ceiling).
- Clean blades monthly with a damp cloth. Dust buildup unbalances the blades and reduces airflow.
- Tighten blade screws annually. Vibration loosens them over time.
