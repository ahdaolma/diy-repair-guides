## Why Paint Instead of Replace
![Sanding kitchen cabinet doors before painting](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.bV5IKGbzU406G-i564FLtgHaNK%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=4741a3fb8dd5a127ac13e91c3823fafa67487686401101fe254f7577d83ce4e3&ipo=images)

New kitchen cabinets cost $5,000–$25,000 installed. Painting existing cabinets costs $150–$400 in materials and a weekend of labor. The National Association of Realtors reports that a kitchen update—of which cabinet painting is the most impactful single change—recovers approximately 75–80% of its cost in home value. Professionally painted cabinets cost $3,000–$6,000. DIY saves roughly $2,500–$5,500.

The key to a finish that looks factory-applied, not brushed: prep is 80% of the work, paint choice is 15%, technique is 5%. Rush the prep and no paint on earth will produce a smooth, durable result.

## Step 1: Remove Doors, Drawers, and Hardware
![Painting cabinet doors with a roller](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse3.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.TPELKVCIu882coKCrUAluQHaDd%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=d18f6b5a96b53df84d70fa78aca6d56eac11c8062aab09d7baf0b3f9d413eabe&ipo=images)

1. Label every door and drawer with numbered painter's tape on the hinge edge. Label the corresponding cabinet opening with the same number. Hinges are adjusted to specific doors—mixing them up turns reassembly into a puzzle.
2. Remove all hardware: hinges, knobs, pulls. Place hardware from each door in a labeled plastic bag.
3. Set up a painting station: sawhorses with 2×4 rails, or a dedicated workbench, in a well-ventilated area (garage, basement with open windows). Doors will be laid flat for painting and need to stay undisturbed for hours.

## Step 2: Clean—Aggressively

Kitchen cabinets accumulate years of cooking grease, skin oil, and food residue. Paint will not bond to grease—it will fisheye (form craters) or peel in sheets within months.

1. Scrub every surface with TSP or Krud Kutter degreaser and a Scotch-Brite pad or stiff brush. Wear rubber gloves—TSP is caustic.
2. Pay particular attention to areas above and around the stove, and the areas people grip to open doors and drawers. These are the greasiest.
3. Rinse with clean water and a clean sponge. The rinse water should run clear, not brown or gray.
4. Let dry completely.

## Step 3: Sand

Sanding serves two purposes: it removes the gloss from the existing finish (creating mechanical adhesion for primer) and it smooths surface imperfections. Paint adheres to a sanded surface far better than to a glossy one.

1. Sand all surfaces with 120-grit sandpaper or sanding sponges. You are scuffing, not stripping—the goal is a uniformly dull surface, not bare wood. For oak cabinets with heavy grain: the grain texture will show through paint. If you want a smooth, grain-free finish, fill the grain with a grain filler product (Aqua Coat) before priming. This adds a full day to the project.
2. Pay attention to detail areas: corners, panel edges, and around hinge mortises.
3. Vacuum all sanding dust. Then wipe every surface with a tack cloth. The slightest dust particle becomes a visible bump in the painted finish.

## Step 4: Prime
![Finished painted kitchen cabinets](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse4.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.XOSm4sF8S8N3B1NI8mwQRwHaEK%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=eb91aad995f9c15c21cfc31ddc6e78d94948e078770e484a6288fdad07bf7aeb&ipo=images)

Use a high-bond primer—not a standard wall primer. The primer must bond to the old finish and provide a surface the paint can adhere to. Standard latex primer will peel.

- **Zinsser BIN (shellac-based):** The gold standard. Bonds to any surface, blocks stains and wood tannins (prevents yellow bleed-through on oak), dries in 45 minutes. Smells intensely—use with ventilation and a respirator. $22–28 per quart.
- **Zinsser Stix (water-based urethane):** Nearly as good as BIN, less odor, water cleanup. Slightly longer dry time. $20–25 per quart.

1. Apply one coat of primer to all surfaces using a brush for corners and panel edges, and a 4-inch foam or microfiber roller for flat areas. Roller applies a smoother finish than a brush.
2. Tip off roller stipple immediately: after rolling a section, lightly drag a nearly-dry brush over the wet primer in one direction to smooth the texture.
3. Let primer dry per label. BIN: 45 minutes. Stix: 2 hours.
4. Sand the primer lightly with 220-grit sandpaper to remove any raised grain or dust nibs. Wipe with a tack cloth.
5. Apply a second coat of primer for maximum adhesion and to ensure complete coverage. Sand again.

## Step 5: Paint

**Paint choice matters more than for any other project.** Use a paint formulated for cabinets and trim: Benjamin Moore Advance (water-based alkyd, satin or semi-gloss), Sherwin-Williams Emerald Urethane Trim Enamel, or Fine Paints of Europe Eurolux. These paints level to a hard, smooth, furniture-like finish. Standard wall paint is too soft—it will stick, scratch, and wear through on cabinet doors within months. Do not use standard latex wall paint on cabinets.

1. Apply the first coat. Brush corners, panel edges, and detail areas. Roll flat surfaces immediately. Tip off with a brush.
2. Let dry completely. Cabinet paints dry to the touch in 1–2 hours but need 8–16 hours between coats for proper curing.
3. Sand lightly between coats with 320-grit sandpaper or a fine sanding sponge. The goal is to remove dust nibs, not to cut through the paint. Wipe with a tack cloth.
4. Apply a second coat. For dark colors over light primer, or for maximum durability, apply a third coat.
5. The finish will look slightly textured while wet (orange peel from the roller). It will level as it dries if you used a self-leveling cabinet paint.

## Step 6: Cure—Wait Before Reinstalling

Cabinet paint, especially water-based alkyds like Benjamin Moore Advance, takes 7–14 days to fully cure. It is dry to the touch in hours but soft and easily damaged for at least a week.

- Wait at least 24 hours before reinstalling doors and hardware.
- Do not clean the cabinets for at least 7 days.
- Be gentle with the finish for 2 weeks—the paint will continue to harden over 30 days.
- Do not slam doors during the curing period. The paint at the door edges is the thinnest and most vulnerable to chipping.

## Maintenance

Clean painted cabinets with a damp cloth and mild dish soap. Avoid abrasive cleaners, scrub pads, and ammonia-based glass cleaners—they dull the finish. Touch up chips with a small artist's brush and the saved paint, feathering the edges. Keep a small jar of the original paint sealed and labeled for future touch-ups.
